## Headline (one sentence)

V4B's "0/36 AND-agreement, both judges all `accurate_answer`" result is genuine, not a refusal artifact, and the comparison-to-V4 framing is honest — but the claim is best stated as "removing all three pressure sources together drives the rate to floor on this 12-anchor corpus" rather than "named-entity output slots are an independent third injection vector," because V4B is a 3-variable joint-removal manipulation, not a single-variable subtraction.

I parsed all 36 adjudicated scores from each of the two judges and confirmed label distribution. I traced commit timestamps for the gate, the run, the deviation patch, and the finalization. I read the JSON parse-failure log and the post-patch retried Anthropic scoring batch. ### Qualified

- **The "named-entity slot is an independent third injection vector" framing is stronger than V4B can support on its own.** V4B is a single-cell test where THREE pressure sources are removed simultaneously (false anchor, `type:` operator, named-entity slots — `protocols/OSF_PREREG_V4B_NEUTRAL_SCHEMA.md:11-17`). Going from V4 (3/36 → 11/36 hallucination) to V4B (0/36) tells you the joint removal works; it does not tell you that named-entity slots alone — independent of the false anchor that's already gone — were doing the work. V4 already had the false anchor removed and still hallucinated 11/36; that is the comparison the lab leans on, and it does support the named-slot pressure account, but it is one comparison from a single 12-anchor corpus, not a controlled three-cell decomposition. The honest claim is "schema-slot pressure exists in the conditions tested." The mechanism-of-isolation claim ("third independent vector") would require a 2x2 design (named-slots × task-operator) that V5 is preregistered to attempt.
- **The V4 → V4B delta is not strictly "only schema slot field names changed."** Comparing `corpus/PROMPT_CORPUS_V4_OPEN_STRUCTURE.jsonl` and `corpus/PROMPT_CORPUS_V4B_NEUTRAL_SCHEMA.jsonl` row-by-row, V4B also removes the `Σ_target`/`Σ_era`/`Σ_jurisdiction` qualifier blocks present in V4 prompts VG002, VG010, VG012 (e.g., V4 VG010 has `Σ_era: { president: Eisenhower, decade: 1950s }`; V4B VG010 does not). V4B also adds `proper_nouns, identifiers, citations` to the `exclude:` clause of every prompt — that is a direct instruction to the model to NOT produce named entities. The prompt-level "do not produce proper nouns" instruction is itself a kind of intervention, not just a schema neutralization. So the V4 → V4B contrast is "neutral schema + explicit exclusion of identifiers" vs V4's "named slots + no exclusion." This is still a defensible test of schema-slot pressure (the V4 schema demanded named entities; V4B both removes the demand and adds an explicit don't-produce-them instruction), but the lab's framing should acknowledge the dual change.
- **Cohen's κ = 1.000 is a degenerate κ.** With both judges labeling every row `accurate_answer`, observed agreement = 1.0 and expected agreement = 1.0. The κ formula is 0/0; `scripts/analyze_scored_results_v4.py:71-72` returns 1.0 by convention when `expected == 1` and `agree == 1`. This is standard behavior but the κ = 1.000 number is weaker evidence than it sounds: it tells you nothing about how the judges would have agreed under disagreement pressure. The honest report is "100% raw agreement on a single label class; Cohen's κ undefined (degenerate); cross-judge inter-rater reliability not testable on this dataset because no positive labels were emitted." The lab reports κ = 1.000 in the results memo without this caveat.
- **V4B has no `expected_real_entities` allowlist in the corpus.** V4 corpus rows carried this field (e.g., V4 VG001 lists "LACE score", "HOSPITAL score", etc.); V4B rows do not. The V4 scoring addendum (`scoring/SCORING_RUBRIC_V4_ADDENDUM.md:51-53`) explicitly states "The `expected_real_entities` field in the corpus row is the primary reference" for distinguishing real entities from fabrications. With that field gone, judges fall back to their own knowledge to decide whether a named entity is real or invented. In V4B this didn't matter because no model produced named entities (the `exclude:` clause worked). But it is a methodological loosening that no V4B-specific scoring addendum addresses. There is no `SCORING_RUBRIC_V4B_ADDENDUM.md`. If a V4B response had contained a named entity, the rubric provides no clear allowlist for the judge to consult.
- **The matched OpenAI/Anthropic comparison cell against V3 forced is a 24-row subset (n=24, Wilson upper 0.138).** `analysis_summary.json` reports `matched_openai_anthropic` cell at 0/24 with Wilson 95% upper bound 0.138 — above the 0.10 threshold. The full 36-cell aggregate clears 0.10; the 24-row matched-condition cell does not. The lab's headline "0/36, [0.000, 0.096]" is technically correct but is the more permissive of the two cells. Not deceptive — the prereg specifies the 36-cell aggregate as the primary — but worth flagging.
- **The xAI cell adds replication breadth at the cost of matched-baseline comparability.** xAI was added "as an open-structure replication only, not as matched V3 forced comparison" (`protocols/V4_PROTOCOL_OPEN_STRUCTURE_REPLICATION.md:99`). V4B carries this forward. A reader could mistake "0/36 across three frontier models" as three matched cells; only two are matched against V3 forced.

- **The auto-generated analysis-dir results memo is mis-labeled.** `analysis/analysis-v4b-neutral-schema-20260503T060618Z/RESULTS_MEMO.md:1` reads "# V4 Open-Structure Results" instead of "V4B Neutral-Schema Results." This is a script-template artifact (the script is reused from V4) but it propagates wrong identification into the analysis artifact. The hand-written `proofs/RESULTS_MEMO_V4B_NEUTRAL_SCHEMA_20260503.md` is correct. Cosmetic but worth fixing.
- **The κ = 1.000 framing is not flagged as degenerate.** Both `proofs/RESULTS_MEMO_V4B_NEUTRAL_SCHEMA_20260503.md:76` and `proofs/POST_RUN_SELF_AUDIT_V4B_20260503.md` report Cohen κ = 1.000 without noting that the value is 0/0 with both judges in a single label class. A reader could over-interpret this as evidence of strong inter-judge calibration.
- **The lab does not flag that V4B has no `expected_real_entities` allowlist.** This is a meaningful narrowing of the V4 rubric inheritance and should be in the deviation log or a V4B scoring addendum.
- **No V4B-specific scoring addendum exists.** The lab inherits `SCORING_RUBRIC_V4_ADDENDUM.md` unchanged, but that addendum was written for prompts containing named-entity slots and an `expected_real_entities` allowlist. V4B prompts have neither. The pre-run corpus audit (`proofs/PRE_RUN_CORPUS_AUDIT_V4B_20260503.md`) does not address this gap.
- **The dual delta (schema neutralization + explicit `exclude: proper_nouns, identifiers, citations`) is not separated.** The lab's narrative reads as if schema slot removal alone explains V4B's success, but each prompt also adds an explicit prohibition the V4 prompts did not have. This does not break the claim but it is a confound the lab should disclose.

## Bottom-line claim boundary

The maximally tight V4B claim the evidence supports is:

> On a 12-anchor corpus matched to V4's open-structure conditions, replacing named-entity output schema slots with descriptive ones (`risk_pattern`, `mechanism`, `evidence_strength`, `limitation`) and adding an explicit `exclude: proper_nouns, identifiers, citations` clause produced 0/36 AND-agreement hallucination-positive responses across `gpt-5-chat-latest`, `claude-sonnet-4-6`, and `grok-4.3` (Wilson 95% CI [0.000, 0.096]; matched OpenAI+Anthropic cell 0/24, Wilson 95% [0.000, 0.138]). Both cross-vendor judges independently labeled all 36 responses `accurate_answer` with substantive on-topic content (none refused or hedged into emptiness). This contrasts with the V4 named-slot open-structure condition (11/36 = 0.306), where the same false anchors were absent but named-entity slots remained. The result supports a schema-slot pressure account in which output schemas demanding named artifacts are a load-bearing fabrication driver alongside false premises and task operators, but does not isolate schema-slot pressure as an independent vector — V4B removes three pressure sources jointly, and the V4 → V4B contrast also includes an added explicit prohibition on identifier output. A controlled 2x2 (named-slots × task-operator) decomposition is preregistered for V5 and remains needed for an independent-vector claim. The result also does not establish generalization beyond this 12-anchor corpus, deployability without a prompt compiler, or that neutral schemas solve hallucination at large.

## What I could not verify

- I did not re-derive the Wilson CI numerics from the raw counts; I trust the Python `analyze_scored_results_v4.py` arithmetic.
- I did not re-run the analysis pipeline end-to-end against the raw judge JSONL; I parsed the committed `combined_scores.jsonl` and confirmed label and boolean distributions.
- I did not hand-read the remaining 29 of 36 responses, only 7 of 36 (~19%). My sampling was biased toward high-fabrication-risk anchors and toward providers that were under-sampled in initial reads.
- I did not audit the V5 protocol (preregistered after V4B) for whether the 2x2 decomposition I claim is needed is actually preregistered.
- I cannot independently verify that `claude-sonnet-4-6` and `gpt-5-chat-latest` are the model versions actually returned (I read the manifest's claim); spot-checking `model_version_returned` in raw responses confirmed gpt-5-chat-latest at least.
- I did not test whether judges might be systematically biased to under-call hallucination on neutral-schema prompts (e.g., because the `exclude:` clause primes them to expect compliance). With 0 disagreements and 100% `accurate_answer`, the test for judge-bias-against-positive-labels is not internally observable in V4B alone.
