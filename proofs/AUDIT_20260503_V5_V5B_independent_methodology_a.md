## Verdict

**V4B_LOOP_PARTIALLY_CLOSED**

Five of six pre-run corrections from the V4B audit landed cleanly. Of the three substantive V5-design recommendations (full 2x2 decomposition, token-length-matched controls, dose-response on slot count), V5+V5B delivered **3 of 4 cells of the 2x2** (named-no-exclude, neutral-with-exclude, neutral-no-exclude) and skipped the other two recommendations entirely (no length-matched controls, no dose-response). The chain V4 -> V5(named) -> V5(neutral-with-exclude) -> V5B(neutral-no-exclude) is well-designed for what it tests, the 0/36 results survive hand-read, and the reported claims stay inside what the design supports, but the V4B loop is not fully closed because the named-WITH-exclude cell is missing and the length confound is acknowledged but not controlled. Bounded as-is; not yet a full mechanism result.

## Headline (one sentence)

V5 + V5B genuinely tightened the V4B claim by adding the missing same-run paired comparison (V5: named 10/36 vs neutral-with-exclude 0/36) and by knocking out the explicit-exclusion-clause confound (V5B: neutral-no-exclude 0/36 = same as neutral-with-exclude), giving 3 of 4 cells of the V4B-audit-requested 2x2; the lab acknowledges the missing 4th cell (named-WITH-exclude) and the unfixed token-length confound in `proofs/V4B_V5_FEEDBACK_RECONCILIATION_20260503.md` but did not run dose-response, so the claim remains "schema-slot pressure account on this 12-anchor corpus" rather than a complete mechanism finding.

## What V5/V5B missed

**1. The 4th cell of the 2x2 (named slots WITH explicit exclude clause) was not run.** The V4B audit synthesis (`proofs/AUDIT_SYNTHESIS_V4B_20260503.md:115-117`) named this cell explicitly: `{named slots, neutral slots} × {no exclude clause, with exclude clause}` = 4 cells. V5 + V5B fill 3 cells (named-no-exclude = 10/36; neutral-no-exclude = 0/36; neutral-with-exclude = 0/36). The missing cell is **named slots WITH exclude clause**. Why this matters: it's the cell that answers "does adding `exclude: proper_nouns, identifiers, citations` to a named-slot prompt rescue it, or does the slot label semantics itself drive fabrication?" The lab acknowledges the gap honestly at `proofs/V4B_V5_FEEDBACK_RECONCILIATION_20260503.md:90-98` ("That cell would answer whether explicit exclusions rescue named slots. It is not needed to preserve the current V5/V5B claim, but it is the next clean cell..."). Correct that it is not needed for the *current* claim, but it IS what the V4B audit said V5 should do; the lab quietly substituted "address the explicit-exclusion confound from one direction" (V5B) for "decompose the joint manipulation into a 2x2." Without the named-with-exclude cell, the claim "schema slot semantics is what's doing the work" is supported only by inference, not by the missing-cell observation that would close it.

**2. No dose-response on number of named-entity slots.** Auditor B's strongest V5 recommendation was a 0/1/2/4/8 named-slot dose-response. V5/V5B did not attempt this. The lab concedes the gap explicitly in `proofs/V4B_V5_FEEDBACK_RECONCILIATION_20260503.md`. Why this matters: dose-response converts the binary contrast (10/36 vs 0/36) into a curve that distinguishes "one named slot is enough to flip into named-entity mode" from "fabrication scales with slot count."

**3. Token-length parity is measured but not controlled.** I computed corpus-side prompt lengths: V5 named = 230.2 chars / 25.1 words mean; V5 neutral-with-exclude = 279.0 chars / 26 words; V5B neutral-no-exclude = 239.5 chars / 23 words. The neutral-with-exclude prompts are systematically ~21% longer than named in characters. The lab correctly measures this in `proofs/V4B_V5_FEEDBACK_RECONCILIATION_20260503.md:42-58` and labels it "a mild confound" but did not build a length-matched cell into V5. V5B is closer in length to V5 named (239.5 vs 230.2 chars) — so V5B partially controls the length confound for the no-exclude condition by accident — but neither V5 named-vs-neutral nor V5B contains a deliberately length-matched control. This is a Auditor B caveat (`proofs/AUDIT_20260503_V4B_independent_novelty_b.md:191`) the lab acknowledged but did not fix.

**4. The V5 prereg's H1 threshold uses a difference test, not a per-cell test, and the threshold was effectively trivial to clear.** `protocols/OSF_PREREG_V5_SCHEMA_SLOT.md:20-22`: "named-minus-neutral difference >= 0.15" AND "neutral rate <= 0.10". With V4B already having shown neutral = 0/36 and V4 having shown named = 11/36, the named-minus-neutral effect was already known to be ~0.30. The threshold was preregistered, but it was a known clear in advance from V4+V4B. This is not dishonest — the prereg is a falsifiable contract — but it does mean V5 was confirmation rather than risk. The V5B threshold (`<= 0.10`) was the one that actually exposed the lab to a falsification risk (if V5B had hit 1+/36, the schema-slot mechanism story would have weakened substantially); V5B passing at 0/36 is the more informative single-experiment result of the two.

**5. The human-adjudication-on-disagreement clause from V1/V4B is NOT in either V5 or V5B prereg.** I grep'd both preregs for "human" and "adjud"; only one hit, at `protocols/OSF_PREREG_V5_SCHEMA_SLOT.md:74` ("Misrecall and disagreement logs."), which references a log not a clause. The V4B audit (`proofs/AUDIT_20260503_V4B_independent_claim_boundary_a.md:60`) credited V4B for restoring this clause from V1 after V3 silently dropped it. V5 and V5B silently dropped it again. With 8 V5 disagreements and 1 V5B disagreement, the AND-agreement rule kept all of them as hallucination-negative without human override — which is consistent with what the rule says, but the safety-net the V1 prereg specified is not on the V5/V5B record. This is a quiet methodology drift the lab itself caught and disclosed in V4B but did not preserve in V5/V5B.

**6. The V5 "named-minus-neutral" framing in the primary memo treats V5 as if it isolates schema-slot semantics, with the confound caveat as a footnote.** `proofs/RESULTS_MEMO_V5_SCHEMA_SLOT_20260503.md:8-12` opens "V5 supports the schema-slot mechanism hypothesis. Holding false-anchor absence and `type:` absence constant, named-entity output schemas produced more hallucination-positive responses than neutral descriptive schemas." The exclusion-clause confound caveat appears at lines 53-69 ("Important Caveat") but a casual reader looking at the headline plus per-model tables will leave with the stronger reading. V5B was supposed to retire this caveat, and largely does — but the V5 memo itself was published with the confound still live. The honest read is that the V5 memo is the wrong artifact to read alone; you need V5+V5B together.

## The new claim boundary in one paragraph

On a 12-anchor synthetic corpus across `gpt-5-chat-latest`, `claude-sonnet-4-6`, and `grok-4.3` at temperature 0, with false anchors and `type:` task operators absent in every cell, named-entity output schema slots (`study_name`, `rule_identifier`, `benchmark_name`, `advisory_id`, `statute_name`, `mission_name`) produced 10/36 AND-agreement hallucination-positive responses (Wilson 95% [0.158, 0.440]) — replicating V4's 11/36 to within one row in a same-run paired design — while neutral descriptive output schema slots (`risk_pattern`, `mechanism`, `evidence_strength`, `limitation`) produced 0/36 (Wilson 95% [0.000, 0.096]) both with explicit `proper_nouns, identifiers, citations` exclusions (V5 neutral arm) and without them (V5B). Both judges (cross-vendor, AND-agreement primary rule) flagged real fabrications in the named arm (e.g., invented "SEC-AI-2023-01" rule, invented "Alone Together" sociology study, invented "Avoid Reward Tampering (ART) Tasks" benchmark) and the neutral-arm responses are substantively useful (concrete mechanisms with real-where-applicable citations), not bland generalities. This supports a schema-slot pressure account in which the lexical semantics of output schema field labels is a load-bearing fabrication driver on this corpus, independent of the explicit anti-identifier exclusion clause but dependent on a 12-anchor corpus the lab itself constructed; it does not isolate schema-slot semantics from prompt-length differences (~21% longer characters in the V5 neutral-with-exclude condition; partially controlled in V5B but not by deliberate length-matching), it does not establish dose-response (whether one named slot is sufficient or fabrication scales with slot count), it does not test the missing 4th cell (named slots WITH explicit exclusion), it does not generalize beyond the 12-anchor corpus or three model versions tested, and it does not establish deployability of a prompt-linter product without a real-world schema test.
