# V3 Auditor B: Public Technical Retention Note

Date: 2026-05-02

The original Auditor B memo mixed technical assessment with internal direction
planning. The public repository retains the technical conclusions and removes
that planning material.

## Retained Technical Conclusions

V3 tested a verification-gate prompt pattern. The audit concluded that V3 was
best treated as a small-sample re-derivation of an existing verification-before-
answering pattern rather than as a novel hallucination result.

The audit also identified two discipline issues:

- same-vendor judging weakened the independence of the score;
- the human-adjudication-on-disagreement clause had been dropped from the run
  record.

Those findings directly shaped later lab practice. V4B restored the
human-adjudication clause, and later audits repeatedly checked whether the
clause stayed present.

## Effect On Later Work

The lab moved from V3's verification-gate framing to schema-slot pressure as
the more specific experimental question. V4, V4B, V5, V5B, V5C, and V6 are the
relevant follow-on chain.
